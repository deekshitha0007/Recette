Thekua Recipe - Jharkhand

Procedure

  1. Prepare the Jaggery Syrup
    1. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan.
    2. Add grated jaggery and cook until it melts completely.
    3. Cook until it reaches one-string consistency, then cool slightly.
  2. Make the Dough
    1. Mix wheat flour and semolina in a large bowl.
    2. Add fennel powder, cardamom powder, and grated coconut.
    3. Pour the jaggery syrup and mix well.
    4. Add milk gradually to form a stiff dough.
  3. Shape the Thekua
    1. Divide dough into small portions and roll into balls.
    2. Press each ball with your palm or use a thekua mold.
    3. Create decorative patterns with a fork or knife.
    4. Let them rest for 15 minutes.
  4. Fry and Serve
    1. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
    2. Deep fry thekua on medium heat until golden brown.
    3. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
    4. Cool completely and store in airtight containers.

Enjoy the traditional Jharkhand sweet Thekua!

Main Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Wheat Flour
  • 1/2 cup Semolina (Suji)
  • 1 cup Jaggery (grated)
  • 1/4 cup Fresh Grated Coconut
  • 1/4 cup Ghee
  • 1/4 cup Milk
  • 1 tsp Fennel Powder (Saunf)
  • 1/2 tsp Cardamom Powder
  • Pinch of Salt
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Dry fruits for garnish (optional)
